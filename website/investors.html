Yet the fertilizer industry has never produced a single product designed for indoor use. Every product on the shelf, Miracle-Gro, Jobe's, Espoma, Osmocote, was built for farms, lawns, and outdoor gardens, then cross-marketed to indoor buyers without modification. BioPod occupies the gap between convenience and sustainability that no other product fills.

The product

Pre-measured, multi-compartment, zero burn risk, biodegradable

Each BioPod is a 10-gram hexagonal pod containing organic inputs in four engineered compartments, sealed in a biodegradable cellulose shell. The user places one pod near plant roots and waters normally. The shell dissolves in 30 to 90 seconds, the nutrients release over 8 to 10 weeks in staged phases, and nothing remains in the soil. A $12.99 twelve-pack feeds one indoor plant for a full year.

No other commercially available product combines multiple organic inputs with beneficial soil microbes in a pre-measured, biodegradable, multi-compartment pod. The provisional application covers the multi-compartment architecture, the staged-release mechanism, and the biological delivery system. Replicating it requires simultaneously matching formulation, compartment design, shell material, and biological mechanism.

Validation

Built on evidence, not assumptions

Customer validation

An unbiased customer survey using open-ended questions, conducted before showing respondents the product, found strong purchase intent and willingness to pay in the target range. The survey was designed to avoid leading questions.

Product development

Two working prototypes completed. Three shell materials tested through hands-on bench-scale experimentation, with $2,500 personally invested. Every ingredient individually supported by published peer-reviewed scientific literature.

Current status

Controlled greenhouse trials and chemical nutrient release analysis in progress. Louisiana LLC filed and approved. A provisional application filed with the USPTO. Written confirmation that no institution holds an IP claim. Live website and waitlist operational.
